Moderate-to-Large-x Gluon Helicity from J/ψ Production at √s = 27 GeV
May 29, 2026

📄 New Preprint on arXiv!

Title: Moderate-to-Large-x Gluon Helicity from J/ψ Production at √s = 27 GeV Authors: Shubham Sharma (Moscow, ITEP & MIPT), Alexey Aparin (Moscow, ITEP & MIPT & JINR & Inst. Nucl. Phys., Almaty), Satyajit Puhan (Taiwan, Inst. Phys.), Narinder Kumar (M.N. College), Harleen Dahiya (Ambedkar, Nat. Inst. Technol.) e-Print: 2605.30945 [hep-ph] Pages: 5 pages

Key Highlights

  • 🔹 First projection for constraining moderate-to-large-x gluon helicity distribution Δg(x) via J/ψ production at NICA-SPD energy
  • 🔹 Color-singlet model at LO in perturbative QCD used to compute longitudinal double-spin asymmetry (A_LL)
  • 🔹 Five NLO helicity PDF sets evaluated — NNPDFpol1.1, DSSV14, JAM17, LSS10, and AAL19
  • 🔹 A_LL as a function of p_T and rapidity — significant discriminating power between different helicity PDF scenarios
  • 🔹 Targeted regime: moderate-to-large-x (x ≳ 0.1), poorly constrained by existing RHIC and EIC data

Significance

This work establishes J/ψ production at √s = 27 GeV as a powerful probe for constraining the gluon helicity distribution, directly relevant for the upcoming SPD experiment at the NICA collider facility.

Final PhD Viva Voce — Tomography of Light & Heavy Mesons Using Light-Front Dynamics
2nd April, 2026

🎓 Final PhD Viva Voce — Successfully Defended!

Thesis Title: Tomography of Light and Heavy Mesons Using Light-Front Dynamics Candidate: Satyajit Puhan Supervisor: Dr. Harleen Dahiya Institution: Dr. B R Ambedkar National Institute of Technology, Jalandhar Date: 2nd April, 2026

Thesis Overview

The thesis presents a comprehensive investigation of the internal structure of light and heavy mesons (pion, kaon, ρ-meson, charmonium, bottomonium) using the Light-Front Quark Model (LFQM). Key contributions include:

  • Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs): Leading-twist and subleading-twist PDFs with NLO/NNLO DGLAP evolution
  • Transverse Momentum Dependent Distributions (TMDs): Complete sets of leading-twist TMDs including tensor-polarized TMDs for spin-1 mesons
  • Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs): Full extraction of chiral-even and chiral-odd GPDs
  • Gravitational Form Factors (GFFs): Mass A(t) and D-term D(t) form factors revealing the mechanical structure of hadrons
  • Nuclear Medium Effects: Investigation of meson properties in symmetric nuclear medium at various baryonic densities

Defense Highlights

The defense featured live interactive visualizations built for this research:

  • αₛ(Q²) Running Coupling Constant — Interactive 1-loop, 2-loop, and 3-loop pQCD evolution with real-time parameter adjustment
  • Pion Mechanical Structure — Visualization of pressure distributions, mass form factors, and D-term form factors in the LFQM

Publications

The thesis work has resulted in 29 publications in leading peer-reviewed journals including Physical Review D, JHEP, Physics Letters B, Nuclear Physics B, PTEP, European Physical Journal, and Chinese Physics C.

With deep gratitude to my supervisor Dr. Harleen Dahiya, my collaborators, and the entire research group at NIT Jalandhar.

Internal Structure of Light Mesons Using the Power Law Wave Function — Accepted in Modern Physics Letters A (Special Issue)
1st April, 2026

Title: Internal Structure of Light Mesons Using the Power Law Wave Function Authors: Satyajit Puhan, Narinder Kumar, Harleen Dahiya Journal: Modern Physics Letters A (MPLA), World Scientific Special Issue Guest Editor: Dr. Parada Hutauruk Accepted: April 1, 2026

Summary

We study the internal structure of the pion and kaon using spin-improved power-law wave functions within the light-front framework. Key results include:

  • Distribution Amplitudes (DAs): Leading-twist DAs evolved using LO ERBL equations; pion DA symmetric and consistent with asymptotic result 6x(1−x)
  • Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs): Unpolarized GPDs at zero skewness; pion symmetric, kaon asymmetric due to heavier strange quark
  • Transverse Momentum Dependent Distributions (TMDs): Leading-twist unpolarized TMD f₁(x,k⊥²) for both mesons
  • Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs): NLO DGLAP evolved to Q² = 16 GeV²; only 41% of longitudinal momentum carried by quarks, with 60% carried by gluons
  • Form Factors: Charge form factors in good agreement with NA7, FNAL, and JLab experimental data
  • Charge Radii: r_π = 0.668 fm and r_K = 0.704 fm

Decay Constants

  • Pion: 114 MeV (PDG: 130.2±1.7 MeV, error: 12.44%)
  • Kaon: 144 MeV (PDG: 156.1±0.5 MeV, error: 7.69%)

Recent Paper Published in Physical Review D
27 Feb, 2026

Title: Valence quark distribution of the rho meson using a light-front quark model
Authors: Satyajit Puhan, Shubham Sharma, Narinder Kumar, Harleen Dahiya
Journal: Physical Review D 113, 036030 (2026)
Published: Feb 27, 2026
DOI: 10.1103/xjkc-l5tr

Abstract: We investigate the partonic structure of the ρ meson, the lightest spin-1 vector meson, within the light-front quark model (LFQM). To explore the sensitivity to model assumptions, we employ two distinct types of spin wave functions in the LFQM. Using light-front helicity wave functions, we derive explicit expressions for the leading-twist and subleading-twist quark parton distribution functions (PDFs), and evolve the leading-twist PDFs to higher scales with next-to-leading order (NLO) Dokshitzer--Gribov--Lipatov--Altarelli--Parisi (DGLAP) evolution. We have also calculated the Mellin moment from the evolved PDFs using a simple neural network frame and compared with available theoretical predictions. Furthermore, we compute the full set of nine leading-twist transverse-momentum-dependent distributions (TMDs) for the valence quark in the ρ meson, including three tensor TMDs that arise from spin-1 tensor polarization of the hadron. Positivity constraints for the PDFs and TMDs are examined within this framework. Our findings highlight the crucial role of tensor polarization in shaping the three-dimensional partonic structure of vector mesons.

Spectroscopy of ρ-meson in symmetric nuclear medium — Accepted in PRD
Jan 16, 2026

Title: Spectroscopy of ρ-meson in symmetric nuclear medium
Authors: Anshu Gautam, Tanisha, Satyajit Puhan, Arvind Kumar, Harleen Dahiya
Date: Jan 16, 2026
e-Print: 2601.11082 [hep-ph]

Abstract (arXiv): In this work, we investigate the behavior of the light vector ρ meson in the presence of a symmetric nuclear medium at zero temperature. We calculate the mass and decay constant of the ρ-meson as well as the leading twist distribution amplitudes (DAs) in the light-front quark model in vacuum, which are further investigated at different baryonic densities. We also predict the Mellin moments of the DAs and decay width of the ρ0 → e+ e- process in both vacuum and medium. The evolution of DAs is carried out by the leading order (LO) Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage method and compared with available predictions. For better understanding of medium effects on ρ-meson, we have also predicted the in-medium charge (Gc(Q²)), magnetic (Gm(Q²)), and quadrupole (Gq(Q²)) form factors. The in-medium charge radii, magnetic moment, and quadrupole moment have also been predicted in this work. We have found that the nuclear medium induces appreciable modifications on the mass, weak decay constant, decay width, and distribution amplitudes of the ρ meson. However, the charge radii, magnetic moment, and quadrupole moment are observed to exhibit weaker sensitivity to changes in baryonic density.

⚛️ What I Do

Research Areas

My primary research focuses on understanding the internal structure of hadrons through modern theoretical frameworks in light-front dynamics.

Transverse Momentum Distributions (TMDs)

Model-dependent leading-twist and subleading-twist TMDs for spin-0 (pion, kaon), spin-1/2 (proton), and spin-1 (ρ meson, heavy vector mesons, photon) hadrons within the light-front quark model. Includes extraction of T-even and T-odd TMDs, Boer–Mulders and Sivers functions, spin densities in transverse momentum space, Wigner distributions, and higher-twist PDFs. Positivity bounds and Lorentz-invariance relations are systematically examined.

Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs)

GPDs for spin-0 (pion, kaon) and spin-1 (ρ meson) hadrons encoding both spatial and momentum correlations. Extraction of electromagnetic (Dirac, Pauli) and gravitational (energy-momentum tensor) form factors via Ji's sum rule. Generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) for pion. Global extraction of pion GPDs from Jefferson Lab charge form factor data using neural networks. Directly relevant for upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and JLab 12 GeV measurements.

Hadronic & Mechanical Properties

Spin-orbit correlations, leading-twist and higher-twist distribution amplitudes (DAs) evolved via ERBL equations. Electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, magnetic moments, and quadrupole moments for vector mesons. Decay constants, mass spectra, and radially excited states of quarkonia (charmonium, bottomonium). Radiative transition form factors of charmonia (J/ψ → η_c γ). Gravitational form factors (D-term), pressure and shear force distributions inside the pion revealing mechanical stability.

Medium Modifications & Extraction

In-medium modification of TMDs, PDFs, GPDs, and form factors in symmetric nuclear matter using LFQM combined with the chiral SU(3) coupled quark-meson-Friedberg–Lee (CQMF) model at finite baryon density. Data-driven extraction of pion TMDs and PDFs from E615 and NA10 Drell–Yan cross-section data via neural network parametrization with DGLAP-evolved distributions. Monte Carlo simulations of charmonia (J/ψ, ψ(2S)) production using Pythia 8312 with color-singlet and color-octet NRQCD mechanisms. Gluon helicity distribution Δg(x) constraints via J/ψ production double-spin asymmetry (A_LL) at NICA-SPD energy (√s = 27 GeV). Transport coefficients using the NJL–Polyakov loop model at finite temperature and chemical potential.
